Batch Reverse Osmosis

Water Desalination

The goal of the research is to refine and validate thermodynamic models of BRO (Batch Reverse Osmosis) and t-BRO to predict the performance of a full-scale system and determine whether the technology is a candidate for pilot-scale testing with sunlight and real groundwater. A successful outcome of the project will demonstrate a thermally-driven batch RO process at lab scale. Furthermore, it will produce data paired with modeling to show the potential achievable energy efficiency versus salinity and recovery.

I was part of the team as a mechanical engineer who was designing the physical model to conduct the experiments. I was also helping to construct a model for the More Water Less Concentrate challenge being hosted by NASA.

SARS-CoV-2 Virus Finder

The goal of this paper was to develop an algorithm that could take a FATSA file and check if it contained the SARS-CoV-2 virus. In bio-informatics and biochemistry, the FASTA format is a text-based format for representing amino acid (protein) sequences, in which nucleotides or amino acids are represented using single-letter codes.

After studying and understanding how scientists found virus strains, I broke down the process into smaller increments. This incremental development process helped me break down the process into small helper functions which could work in conjunction to find the virus strain.

"Moreyball" NBA Computational Analysis

Since the introduction of the three-point shot in 1979, its impact on the game has changed wildly. Initially regarded as an obscure shot that only a few players would be capable of making, it has become one of the most important facets of professional basketball. This impact has especially manifested itself over the last decade, where teams have begun to center their entire offense around shooting three-pointers. This strategy, often referred to as "Moreyball" as a nod to its pioneer, General Manager Daryl Morey, places emphasis on shooting layups and three-pointers, and specifically eliminating long two-point shots (shots that fall right inside of the three-point arc). The reason for this strategy is simple: layups are made at the highest percentage of any shot, and while three-pointers are made at a much lower percentage, the extra point compensates for the lost efficiency.

Using Fourier Transformations to Detect Fallings

FALL De_TECH

Our Fall De_Tech algorithm makes use of the sensors present in a smartphone to detect when someone has fallen. The sensor that we utilized for this project is the accelerometer. Our goal was to collect data of someone walking/running normally with the phone and to collect data of someone falling with the phone in various ways (tripping, stumbling without falling all the way over, rolling, etc.). We then compared the data and used any anomalies present in the falling data to help us understand what to look for in order to tell whether or not someone has fallen.

Using RANSAC Algorithms to Develop Contour Fields

Spatial Recognition Robot

The goal of this project was to self-drive a robot using LIDAR scans in the direction of a target object. LIDAR is a method for determining ranges by targeting an object with a laser and measuring the time for the reflected light to return to the receiver. After collecting the data, we had to translate the scan into objects that the robot could detect and avoid. I was in charge of developing the RANSAC algorithm using orthogonality. Random sample consensus (RANSAC) is an iterative method to estimate parameters of a mathematical model from a set of observed data that contains outliers.
